This project utilizes Tracery, a generative grammar tool, to explore the diverse perspectives and interpretive frameworks surrounding J.R.R. Tolkien’s The Lord of the Rings trilogy. Through a custom grammar structure, the generator produces sentences that reflect how various groups—such as fans, scholars, writers, and psychologists—engage with Tolkien’s mythos. The output highlights recurring themes including heroic archetypes, mythic symbolism, and moral dilemmas, while also connecting these elements to broader human experiences like creative expression, personal growth, and community building. By automating the generation of reflective statements, the project demonstrates how procedural storytelling can illuminate the cultural and psychological impact of Tolkien’s work, offering a dynamic lens for literary scholarship and fan engagement.

Works Cited
